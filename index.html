Portfolio

3D Portfolio

  •

    The Desert Hub is my first large scale outdoor group project. Initially we made a block in of the environment in Maya. The desert hub is city with a post apocalypse concept and its carved from sand rocks. There are many assets in this environment like Sand rocks, Dome, Bridges, cactus factory, garage, elevator, market place and houses etc. I worked on landscaping the city, sculpting the huge sand rocks , the greenhouse dome, waterfall and few street lamps. This project helped us to understand the large scale measurements and texturing for the same. Finally all the assets were imported in Unreal engine 5 to make this environment usable for any game.

    This environment has two versions of textures, toon and realistic version.

  • Japanese living room is my first indoor project. Its really fun to create different props to fulfill the room requirement. I worked on lights, shadows and colors inspired from few references. It is satisfying to see all objects coming together to give it a Japanese touch.

  • As a kid I always adored Harry Potter as super hero. I turned my fantasy to real, while making this character project. This process taught me all different aspects of creating the character model to animation. I have tried to portray my entire process of building this character to life.

  •

    A remote silo scene which was my first outdoor project at school. Through this project I learned different aspects of workflow in 3D modelling. Also, went through several methods of creating realistic textures using the painter baking process. This entire piece of scene is inspired from a 2d illustration.

  •

    This fiction character of Gollum was sculpted in Zbrush initially and then it's retopology was done in maya for the low poly. Later the details were baked in Painter.

  •

    The witch's trunk is an asset that contains several props in it. I worked them out in all different ways to achieve best results. Every tiny prop has been baked with details from high. Its satisfactory to see the different materials coming out as real ones. Also, for a fantasy aspect, I animated them to bring some life in all. This model is inspired from an 2D illustration made by a fellow classmate.

  •

    I made this medieval door to follow a low polycount to high workflow. Its fulfilling to see how the details in textures speaks everything. This door is a full prop ready to be in any game scene.

  • The spiral staircase is a prop made to be used in any of the royal or medieval period castle/house. I followed few general references for the pillar and stairs. Later its imported to unreal to check the game ready asset.

  • I always wanted to make a cute stuff toy model, so as an inspiration I created the model from my collection. The fur are developed using XGen tool. Later I also rendered a cute animation for this little guy.

  • I made this landscape while learning about MASH component of Maya. It really is an amazing tool to work on outdoor environments. Tweaking with few lights I gave it a little twilight look! I did not use any reference image, Its purely an imaginary scene.

  • I always have enjoyed rainy weather. This model is recreation of a rainy night scene. I have tried to work with some lightings and shadows.

  • Inspired from an old designer accent chair, I created this model to make it look like a realistic one.
