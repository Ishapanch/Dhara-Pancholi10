3D Portfolio

    The witch's trunk is an asset that contains several props in it. I worked them out in all different ways to achieve best results. Every tiny prop has been baked with details from high. Its satisfactory to see the different materials coming out as real ones. Also, for a fantasy aspect, I animated them to bring some life in all. This model is inspired from an 2D illustration made by a fellow classmate.

  •

    As a kid I always adored Harry Potter as super hero. I turned my fantasy to real, while making this character project. This process taught me all different aspects of creating the character model to animation. I have tried to portray my entire process of building this character to life.

  •

    This fiction character of Gollum was sculpted in Zbrush initially and then it's retopology was done in maya for the low poly. Later the details were baked in Painter.

  •

    A remote silo scene which was my first outdoor project at school. Through this project I learned different aspects of workflow in 3D modelling. Also, went through several methods of creating realistic textures using the painter baking process. This entire piece of scene is inspired from a 2d illustration.

  •

    I made this landscape while learning about MASH component of Maya. It really is an amazing tool to work on outdoor environments. Tweaking with few lights I gave it a little twilight look! I did not use any reference image, Its purely an imaginary scene.

  • I always have enjoyed rainy weather. This model is recreation of a rainy night scene. I have tried to work with some lightings and shadows.

  • I always wanted to make a cute stuff toy model, so as an inspiration I created the model from my collection. The fur are developed using XGen tool. Later I also rendered a cute animation for this little guy.

  • As a part of my class project, I made this robot model. I used the concept of low polycount and baked major details in textures. Later I rigged the robot and animated to give it a little life.

  • Inspired from an old designer accent chair, I created this model to make it look like a realistic one.

  • I made this medieval door to follow a low polycount to high workflow. Its fulfilling to see how the details in textures speaks everything. This door is a full prop ready to be in any game scene.

  • The spiral staircase is a prop made to be used in any of the royal or medieval period castle/house. I followed few general references for the pillar and stairs. Later its imported to unreal to check the game ready asset.

  • Japanese living room is my first indoor project. Its really fun to create different props to fulfill the room requirement. I worked on lights, shadows and colors inspired from few references. It is satisfying to see all objects coming together to give it a Japanese touch.

  • The Desert Hub is my first large scale outdoor group project. Initially we made a block in of the environment in Maya. The desert hub is city with a post apocalypse concept and its carved from sand rocks. There are many assets in this environment like Sand rocks, Dome, Bridges, cactus factory, garage, elevator, market place and houses etc. I worked on landscaping the city, sculpting the huge sand rocks , the greenhouse dome, waterfall and few street lamps. This project helped us to understand the large scale measurements and texturing for the same. Finally all the assets were imported in Unreal engine 5 to make this environment usable for any game.

    This environment has two versions of textures, toon and realistic version.

Download CV

As a dedicated 3D Artist, I, Dhara Pancholi, bring a rich blend of creativity and technical expertise to the world of 3D modeling. With a keen interest in both fantasy and reality, my work is fueled by a passion for transforming imaginative concepts into visually compelling assets for film and entertainment.

My journey in 3D art began with a fascination for the intricate details that make characters and environments come alive on screen. I specialize in creating high-quality, detailed models ranging from realistic props to fantastical creatures, and immersive environments that capture the essence of the narrative. My approach involves a deep understanding of both artistic principles and technical processes, ensuring that each model not only looks stunning but also performs efficiently within the digital space.

I am skilled in all stages of the 3D modeling pipeline, from initial concept design and low-poly modeling to high-resolution sculpting and texturing. My proficiency with industry-standard software such as Maya, Photoshop, Substance 3D Painter, and ZBrush allows me to produce high-caliber work that meets the demands of modern film and game production.

My goal is to continuously push the boundaries of 3D artistry by integrating innovative techniques and staying abreast of industry trends. I thrive in collaborative environments where creative vision and technical skill converge to create memorable and impactful visual experiences.

    Just because we can't get out and about like we normally would, doesnt mean we have to stop taking pictures. Theres still plenty you can do, provided you're prepared to use some imagination. Here are a few ideas to keep you shooting until normal life resumes.

    Most photographers love to shoot the unusual, and you dont get much more unusual than These Unprecedented Times. Right now everything counts as out of the ordinary. There are a number of remarkable things about these lockdown days that are worth photographing now so we can remember them when it is all over.

    Most photographers find it hard to see interesting pictures in places in which they are most familiar. A trip somewhere new seems always exactly what our photography needed, as shooting away from home consistently inspires us to new artistic heights.

    Streets empty that are usually busy are remarkable and can evoke the sense of historical pictures from before the invention of the motorcar. Other things that are different at the moment will be queues to get into stores and the lines marked out on the floor to show how far apart we should be.

    Pretend everything is new and that you havent seen it before, and then you will be free to notice the leading lines, the places where one edge meets another in delightful geometric harmony, and how the ordinary things in the kitchen are transformed when the light is on or off.

    The trick here is to look slowly, and then look again. Take the time to look in detail and to look at the same thing from different angles, with different light, long lenses and wide lenses. Then move to the left a bit. You may never feel the need to leave the house again.
